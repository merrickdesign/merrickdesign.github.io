Building a video chat feature in Candy Crush Friends.

Goal

King wanted a proof of concept that video chat could be implemented at scale into one of our games. We put our hands up took on the challenge.

The result was a stable build released in 4 major markets and a bootstrap design system that other teams could pick up and start implementing video chat into there games.

Having proved out this feature and supplied design guidelines, we unlocked 2 other teams to implement this feature.

Process

Define functionality and patterns

I looked through the major video calling apps to identify common patterns and define minimum expected functionality. I also researched competitors to see how others may have solved the problem.

flows

Following the research, I ran a session with the team to define the phases and functionality required for users to make a call. This exercise helped determine the screens needed and requirements of each screen. As we moved through development, I replaced the text with lo-fi wires through to final visuals.

Mapping out the functionality needed at each phase of the user journey.

Adding wireframes and defining layouts

Replacing wireframes with HD mockups

Prototypes

I built prototypes for testing and help align the team on how it would feel navigate the feature. Along with providing examples of functionality, such as the full-screen toggle view. We didn't have time to recruit Candy Crush Friends users, so we recruited our colleagues from other departments outside of the game team.

Building a system

Release

We released in 4 major markets successfully prooving King could implement video chat at scale. Having proved out this feature and supplied design guidelines, we unlocked other teams to implement this feature.
